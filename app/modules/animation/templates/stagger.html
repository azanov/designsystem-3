Staggered Animations

When building a staggered animation you have two options. The first is is to use angular's ng-repeat with your animation class name and the class pb-stagger. This is the more preferrable solution. However, there are cases where we want to put a staggered transition on something that isn't a good fit with ng-repeat, such as a button group. For this case, we provide additional classes.

Manual Stagger

Stagger entrances and exits without ng-repeat

In order to set an animation delay in increments wrap the children you want effected in a div or span with one of our stagger classes. You can manually stagger div, li, span, and button elements.

The stagger animations come in three different flavors. Forwards, backwards, and bidirectional.

  • forwardStagger: Forward stagger will always animate from left to right or top to bottom, regardless of whether you are animating in or out.
  • backwardStagger: Backward stagger will always animate from right to left or bottom to top, regardless of whether you are animating in or out.
  • bidirectionalStagger: Bidrectional stagger will animate from left to right or top to bottom when animating in, and right to left or bottom to top when animating out.
