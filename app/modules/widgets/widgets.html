Writing for Containers

When writing headers or labels for container elements, follow these basic principles.

Style

  • Use short, active words and phrases.
  • Structure copy to avoid ambiguity in language.
  • Use the same style and structure for all your labels.
  • To avoid confusing users, use consistent terminology.

Capitalization

  • Use title capitalization for all container headers and labels.
  • Capitalize all words containing four or more letters.

Punctuation

  • Don’t conclude copy with punctuation, such as periods or colons.
  • Don’t phrase your copy as questions.

Word Count

  • Keep word counts to three words or fewer possible.
  • If you need to use more than three words, use the smallest number possible to express the container element’s purpose.
  • “Future proof” your containers by considering if you’ll need to add elements, such as tabs, later on — then choose your wording to allow for those additions.
  • Consider translation needs when choosing words — some languages may require more words or characters to express an idea, which could cause titles in elements such as tab labels to wrap.
