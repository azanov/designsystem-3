Process Indicators

Process indicators show users that they’ll need to wait until a system process completes. The amount of time needed for completion can be determinate or indeterminate — our indicators reflect which status applies.

Indeterminate

We can use animation or descriptive copy — or both — to let users know that a process of indeterminate length is taking place.

Determinate

When the completion time of a process is determinable, we can communicate the user’s wait time (and give them the means to stop the process) in several ways:

  • Use of animation or descriptive copy — or both
  • A countdown of time remaining
  • A “percentage completed” progress bar label
  • A cancel button to halt the operation before completion

Step-Based

We use step-based progress indicators for scenarios in which a user needs to perform a series of tasks, in a step-by-step order, to complete a single, complex process. We have several means by which to communicate progress to the user, by displaying:

  • Which steps have been completed
  • Which step the user is currently performing
  • How many steps remain in the process
  • A final message with clear indication of completion status
