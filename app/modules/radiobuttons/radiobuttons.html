Radio Buttons

Usage

  • When possible, you should use the enhanced style.
  • If your product has long lists of radio buttons (and space is an issue), consider using the standard OS style.
  • Use the same style for your radio buttons and checkboxes, and apply that style consistently across your product.

Styles

We have two styles of radio buttons: enhanced and standard OS.

Writing for UI Controls

When writing your labels, dropdown menu items and in-field prompt copy it’s important to stay consistent across your entire product. Here are some basic guidelines for how to write these critical pieces of user interface copy.

Style

  • Use short, active words and phrases.
  • Structure copy to avoid ambiguity in language.
  • Use the same style and structure for all your labels.
  • To avoid confusing users, use consistent terminology.
  • Introduce a group of controls with copy that clearly defines its purpose.

Capitalization

  • Labels use sentence capitalization, except when copy includes words or phrases that are acronyms or proper nouns, which should be capitalized.
  • Dropdown menu items use title capitalization because they behave like buttons.

Punctuation

  • Don’t conclude copy with punctuation, such as periods or colons.
  • Don’t phrase your copy as questions.

Error Text Length

  • Error text length should be similar in length to the input field associated with it.
  • If your error text needs to be longer than its companion field, consider wrapping/flowing it — bearing in mind that 3-6 columns in width is optimal.
