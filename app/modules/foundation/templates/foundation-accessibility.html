Our color palettes offer numerous options for designers. When it comes to making color choices for on-screen text, however, some colors and color combinations are more readable than others. To ensure that our products are accessible from a readability perspective, follow these guidelines when applying text and background colors.

Colors

This table shows which of our colors are of sufficient contrast to meet the WCAG’s minimum contrast ratios for accessibility. A green checkmark indicates acceptable combinations of foreground text color and our two most common background colors (white and light gray). Note that some combinations are only acceptable when text is a minimum of 14pt+ bolded or 18pt+ regular.


Gradient

Our PB Blue to PB Magenta gradient should be combined only with white text in order to maintain proper color contrast. As you can see in this example, by using white text we can remain well within the WCAG’s minimum contrast ratio even at the lighter end of the gradient.
