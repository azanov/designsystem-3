Usage

Split views are useful for consolidating discrete collections of inputs or other content under a set of secondary navigational elements. If you’re considering using them in your product, keep in mind that split views:

  • Are not responsive
  • Should be used for secondary navigation only
  • Will not persist, meaning they will disappear should the user navigate to another area
  • Aren’t meant for mobile development — they may work on some tablets but will not work on smartphones

Styles

You have a choice of five design variations for split views. Just make sure that the design you choose is consistent with the rest of your product’s visual design.

Types

If your split view areas have sub-menu items, you can choose to display those in an “expanded” view (all sub-menu items visible by default, shown under their section heads) or in a “collapsed” view (all sub-menu items hidden by default, accessed via a “tree” behavior).
